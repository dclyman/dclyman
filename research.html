Below is the presentation poster I made for my research project under the Arnhold Undergraduate Research Fellowship.

POSTER ABSTRACT

This project examines the hypersexualism and male-centricism present within the visual novel Doki Doki Literature Club (2017). It aims to investigate how the horror aspect of the game challenges preconceptions about the genre, and furthermore bringing to light these issues it terms of player identity. The questions posed in this project are: How is the visual novel influence by Japanese pop culture? How does this affect Doki Doki’s story arch? Lastly, how do the creators of Doki Doki challenge other games’ ceonception of player character and identity. While some view this game as a homage to the visual novel canon such as Clannad, it actually shows a tendency to counter this genre’s tropes by destroying the very game world it is presented in.
